The Great Debate: Top-Load vs. Front-Load

Is there really a difference? Other than appearance, there are a few specific distinctions between a top-load and front-load machine.

Top-Load

  • Offers the traditional look and cycle options
  • More convenient to load
  • Less maintenance than front-loading machines
  • In general, less expensive than its front-loading counterparts
  • Also available in HE models
Tip
For added capacity to wash those larger items like comforters and pillows, look for a top-load machine with an impeller instead of an agitator.

Front-Load

  • Removes more water from your load resulting in shorter drying times
  • More cycle types for all of your washing needs
  • Gentler on your washables
  • Quieter than top-loading machines
  • HE/ENERGY STAR compliant saving you water, electricity and money!
Tip
Front-load machines need to be cleaned regularly per manufacturers’ instructions to prevent bacterial growth.

Want to Help Save the Environment and Your Cash?

Check out an HE machine!

But what does high efficiency really mean?
High efficiency washers use up to 80% less water and feature 65% more energy savings than traditional washers. They are ENERGY STAR certified and load capacities are usually larger, which means fewer loads to get through!

Tip
Because HE washers are low-sudsing they require HE detergent. Make sure you check for the HE symbol on your favorite detergent brand.

Additional Features to Up Your Laundry Game

  • Auto-Dispenser System
  • Adds the detergent, bleach and fabric softener automatically for optimal clean and less work for you!
  • Steam Wash
  • Provides a higher level of sanitation perfect for removing allergens. Steam wash machines also help soften your fabrics and remove tough stains.
  • Self-Adjusting Temperature Control
  • Automatically changes water temperatures based on wash settings.
  • End-of-Cycle Signals
  • Sometimes a melodious tune or a short chirp, either way it’ll let you know when your laundry is ready for the dryer.
  • Stainless Steel Wash Tub
  • More durable than traditional porcelain or plastic tubs; because this material only gets smoother over time there is less wear and tear on your clothing.
  • Pedestals
  • This optional front-load accessory lifts your machine off of the ground making your washables easier to load. It also provides extra storage to organize your laundry room.
Tip
Each brand OFFERS its own unique set of features. Always losing that one sock before you press start on your cycle? Samsung® offers AddWash™ this handy door allows you to add that rogue sock after your load has already begun.
